User Awareness Module

Learn how phishing websites work and how to stay safe online.

Phishing is a cyber attack in which criminals create fake websites, messages, or emails to trick users into sharing passwords, banking details, or personal information. User awareness is important because many phishing attacks succeed by targeting human trust rather than technical weaknesses.

Common Signs of Phishing Websites

  • Very long or unusual website addresses
  • Misspelled brand names or fake-looking domains
  • Lack of HTTPS on websites asking for personal details
  • Too many numbers, special symbols, or hyphens
  • Urgent words such as verify, confirm, secure, or update
  • Pages that ask for passwords unexpectedly

Safe Browsing Tips

  • Type important website addresses directly into your browser.
  • Check the main domain name carefully before logging in.
  • Avoid clicking unknown links in emails or text messages.
  • Look for HTTPS before entering passwords or payment details.
  • Use strong passwords and enable two-factor authentication.
  • Keep your browser, operating system, and antivirus updated.

What Not to Do

  • Do not enter your password, banking details, or personal information on a suspicious website.
  • Do not trust a website only because it uses familiar words such as secure or verify.
  • Do not ignore unusual login prompts, urgent warnings, or poor-quality page design.

What To Do When You Find a Suspicious Website

  • Close the page immediately if something looks unsafe.
  • Analyse the URL again using this detection platform or another trusted security tool.
  • Report the suspicious link to your school, company, or service provider.
  • Change your password immediately if you already entered it on a fake website.
